Why Visit Ghana?

Ghana, located in West Africa, offers a rich tapestry of cultural experiences, historical landmarks, and breathtaking natural beauty. From the bustling markets of Accra to the serene beaches along the coast, Ghana welcomes visitors with its famous hospitality.

Rich History

Explore ancient kingdoms and colonial forts that tell the story of Ghana's past.

Natural Beauty

Discover lush rainforests, wildlife reserves, and pristine beaches.

Vibrant Culture

Experience traditional festivals, music, dance, and delicious cuisine.

Travel Tips for Ghana

Visa Requirements

Most visitors to Ghana require a visa, which should be obtained before arrival. Check with the Ghanaian embassy in your country for specific requirements.

Health Precautions

Yellow fever vaccination is mandatory for entry. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended, and it's advisable to drink bottled water.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (November to March) is generally the best time to visit, with less rainfall and more comfortable temperatures.

Currency

The local currency is the Ghanaian Cedi (GHS). Credit cards are accepted in major hotels and restaurants, but cash is essential for markets and smaller establishments.

Cultural Etiquette

Ghanaians value respect and politeness. Use your right hand for greetings and eating. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Transportation

Domestic flights connect major cities. Tro-tros (minibuses) are a common form of public transport. For convenience, consider hiring a driver.
